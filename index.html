Planning a trip to Galway?

Whether you are visiting Galway or whether you are a local who'd like to learn more about Galway's history, Walking Galway History tours are a must for you! Journey on foot through Galway's narrow Medieval streets and delve into the fascinating stories behind some of the city's most iconic landmarks.


Founded in 2022, Walking Galway History tours are run by a small team of local volunteers who are passionate about the long and varied history of Galway. They will be happy to answer your questions as they lead you through Galway's exciting and sometimes shocking past!


When can I take a tour?

Tours normally take place on Tuesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays at 11a.m. from March to October. All tours are free of charge.


Your tour begins at the Browne Doorway at the north end of Eyre Square. Click HERE to view location on Google Maps.


It is sometimes possible to book tours for groups outside of the normal times - please contact us and we will try to facilitate you!


What else do I need to know?

The route of each tour is approximately 2.5km long, and most tours will take about 2 hours.


All routes and buildings on the tour are accessible for people with mobility issues.


It does rain sometimes in Galway...quite a lot, actually! An umbrella is recommended.


You will work up an appetite during the tour - our guides will be happy to recommend local spots for lunch!
